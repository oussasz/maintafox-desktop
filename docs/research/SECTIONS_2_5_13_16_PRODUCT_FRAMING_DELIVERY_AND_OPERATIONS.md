# Sections 2-5 and 13-16 Product Framing, Delivery, and Operations Research Brief

## 1. Research Position

These sections should no longer read like early-stage startup positioning text.

After the hardening of the core modules, Maintafox now has a clearer product identity:

- local-first execution engine
- governed maintenance workflow system
- evidence-grade reliability and cost data platform
- secure desktop control surface with centralized entitlement and operational coordination

The framing, UX, delivery, and VPS sections should therefore express:

- what the product is operationally
- where its trust and authority boundaries live
- how it should be delivered without outrunning its platform maturity
- how operators, administrators, and vendor teams interact with the control plane

## 2. Source Signals

### 2.1 Hardened modules define the real product, not only the marketing copy

The rewritten modules show that Maintafox is no longer merely a desktop port of a CMMS. It is a configurable but governed maintenance operations platform whose value comes from:

- stage-gated workflows
- readiness-aware planning
- auditability
- document and permit control
- telemetry and ERP integration contracts
- protected analytical semantics

The framing sections must now reflect that seriousness.

### 2.2 UX rules follow workflow seriousness

Once the product includes blocked-work queues, dangerous-action controls, acknowledgement requirements, and audit journaling, the desktop UX can no longer be framed as simple CRUD plus dashboards. It needs stronger rules about:

- state visibility
- blocker explanation
- offline and sync status
- role-scoped actions
- drill-through from summaries to evidence

### 2.3 Delivery plans must be risk-gated

The current module set spans local workflows, reliability analytics, sync, licensing, updater, integrations, and vendor control-plane operations. A credible delivery plan must therefore gate releases on:

- platform readiness
- data quality and migration safety
- security controls
- integration contract maturity
- operational observability

## 3. Product and Delivery Implications

### 3.1 Product framing should emphasize local control and governed evidence

The strongest product claim is not simply AI or speed. It is that Maintafox gives industrial teams a local-first operating environment with structured evidence that remains usable offline and analyzable later.

### 3.2 Delivery should move in controlled release trains

The product should be delivered in waves that build:

- secure foundation
- operational execution backbone
- planning, compliance, and intelligence layers
- enterprise control-plane capabilities
- launch hardening and observability

### 3.3 VPS operations should be treated as a control plane

The VPS is not the center of daily work execution. It is the entitlement, update, sync-coordination, and vendor-operations plane.

## 4. Recommended PRD Upgrade Summary

- replace broad product statements with clearer operational identity and measurable objectives
- strengthen UI/UX rules around state, blockers, evidence, and offline behavior
- replace checklist-heavy timeline optimism with gated delivery phases and exit criteria
- strengthen VPS operations around tenancy, backups, observability, rollout control, and `console.maintafox.systems`
